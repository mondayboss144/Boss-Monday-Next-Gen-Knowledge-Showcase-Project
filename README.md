1. THE PROBLEM: THE "DIGITAL BLIND SPOT" 
Despite rapid urban growth in Nigeria, millions of citizens remain "digitally invisible." 
Existing satellite imagery is often outdated or low-resolution, making it impossible for 
telecommunications providers and government agencies like NITDA to accurately plan fiber
optic routes or cell tower placements. This "Digital Blind Spot" creates a massive barrier to 
internet expansion, leaving entire communities excluded from the digital economy. 
2. TARGET USERS 
雞ｷ Telecommunications Companies: To identify precise "line-of-sight" paths for 
microwave and fiber deployment. 
雞ｷ Government Agencies (NITDA/FMCIDE): To identify underserved regions 
requiring urgent digital intervention. 
雞ｷ Urban Planners: To utilize real-time spatial data for organized community 
infrastructure development. 
3. THE BUILD (TOOLS & METHODOLOGY) 
Project Sky-Link leverages a hybrid of hardware and software to create high-fidelity 
Geographic Information Systems (GIS): 
雞ｷ Data Acquisition: High-speed Drones equipped for 4K Photography and 
Videography to capture real-time "ground truth." 
雞ｷ Processing: Windows-based workstations utilizing professional GIS software to layer 
visual data over digital grids. 
雞ｷ Security & Integrity: Applying Cybersecurity principles and Data Auditing 
(refined in healthcare IT environments) to ensure all mapping data is tamper-proof 
and accessed only by authorized stakeholders. 
雞ｷ Collaboration Tools: Google Workspace for project documentation and AI-assisted 
modeling for infrastructure simulations. 
4. IMPACT 
雞ｷ Accelerated Connectivity: Reduces the "cost of discovery" for ISPs by up to 40%, 
directly speeding up internet deployment to unmapped areas. 
雞ｷ Resource Efficiency: Eliminates the need for slow, expensive manual land surveying, 
allowing for rapid infrastructure audits. 
雞ｷ Economic Inclusion: By putting these communities on the map, Sky-Link enables 
residents to access remote work, digital education, and global marketplaces. 
5. SCALABILITY 
The Sky-Link model is designed for national deployment: 
雞ｷ Phase 1: Focused on the dense urban-rural fringes of Lagos. 
雞ｷ Phase 2: Expansion to all 36 states by training a network of 3MTT Fellows as

雞ｷ Phase 3: Integration into a national "Smart City" database managed by NITDA to 
support Nigeria's goal of creating 2 million digital jobs.
